What do gamers want the most?

Gamers want to have fun, they want the kick that got them to play games in the first place. More importantly, they want the games they’re playing to be virtual worlds, complete and comprehensive in themselves.

here can be no thought of finishing for ‘aiming for the stars.’ Both figuratively and literally, it is a task to occupy the generations. And no matter how much progress one makes, there is always the thrill of just beginning.

Many games, like Fortnite and League of Legends, have built themselves on top of paid transactions for characters, rare aesthetic in-game items and currency. Yet the details of how these purchases interact are often unclear. What should players own? How should goods be traded? Who should dictate pricing? The answers depend on the game you play and can vary depending on the developers, sowing distrust and confusion. Players want consistency, flexibility, transparency, and privacy and they’re not getting it.

A Gamers’ usual cry :

  • I can’t get my items from one account to another
  • I wish I could sell my stuff
  • I wish I could sell my stuff
  • I wish I could sell my stuff

These restrictions are imposed by digital distribution intermediary platforms like Steam or Google Play and are the present day status quo.
